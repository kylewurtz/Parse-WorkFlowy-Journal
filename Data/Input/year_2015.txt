              <outline text="W: @Mixed Getting set up for the week; Helping Matthew" >
                <outline text="Thoughts regarding working on the CA team..." />
                <outline text="I personally feel like single state regions are more interesting than the multi-state regions, and I think that CA is probably the most interesting of the single state regions due to the regulatory constraints, the uniqueness of the risks (particularly on property with the wildfires and earthquakes), the uniqueness of the methodologies (mix of state mandated and internal), and the high profile nature of the state. I also personally like that we pretty much never follow a countrywide methodology because it allows us a lot of interesting opportunities to create our own approach to fit each situation. " />
                <outline text="The downside of all of the above is that it can be a pretty intense environment. We've been overworked probably 75% of the time I've been on the team (that's been a consistent sentiment amongst all analysts on the team, so I don't think it's just me) and the stress levels are usually somewhat high. Analysts on the team have also had difficulty passing exams while on the team due to the workload. I think it's possible to keep a manageable workload level on the team, but you just have to be willing to push back on timelines and say &quot;No&quot; to things. That's not very easy to do on a consistent basis, but it's probably a good skill to learn." />
                <outline text="As far as working for Megan goes, I have mixed feelings (please keep this to ourselves). Megan has had to juggle a lot, and as a result her managing can be somewhat inconsistent. One day you'll be asked to make quick assumptions to get a project done quickly and then the next day you'll be asked to add a layer to the project that likely won't add much value and will take several days. I find myself feeling frustrated with the way the team is managed (always being asked to do more work than is appropriate, aforementioned inconsistencies, etc.) quite a bit. That said, a lot of the issues can be alleviated by taking more direct control over your time management...and like I said earlier, that's a good thing to learn. And on the other hand, I've learned a ton from working for Megan. She's very smart and will almost always push you to produce a higher-level work product than you would otherwise. Some might find that annoying, but I like having someone keep me from getting lazy when I start slacking off." />
                <outline text="Ultimately, I think whether it's a right fit will probably come down to a personality thing and where you want to situate work in your life. They usually only bring analysts who are experienced, technical, and a bit creative onto the team, and from what I've seen and heard about your work I definitely don't think there are any concerns there. I think you'd be awesome on the team and I think being on CA is a really incredible development opportunity. The only real downside is that the high-pressure environment can cause some problems...if you're willing to take ownership of your time management and accept that there are going to be some stressful high-workload times, I think CA would be a great fit." />
                <outline text="Let me know if you want to chat more about any of my ramblings or if you have any specific questions!" /></outline>
